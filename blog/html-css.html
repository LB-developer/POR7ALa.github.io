Margin, Border, Padding.

To understand the difference between margin, borders, and padding, it's essential to first grasp the concept of Content. In web design, content refers to the text, images, and other elements that make up a webpage.

Think of content as the core element around which styling is applied. By understanding how margin, padding, and borders work with content, you can better control the layout and spacing of your webpage elements.

All these elements are composed of boxes. Below, you can see a visual representation of how they take shape on a web page.

Differences:

Margin: The space surrounding the content outside of the border. Increasing margin increases the distance between the container and other elements.

Border: The edge or frame of the content, and the divider of the margin and padding.

Padding: The space surrounding the content inside of the border. Increasing padding increases the distance between the content and the border of the box it is contained in.
